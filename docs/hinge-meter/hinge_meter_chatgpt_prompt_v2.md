# Improved ChatGPT Prompt for Hinge Meter (v2)

Copy and paste the entire block below into ChatGPT (or another strong image model).

---

Create a highly precise, semi-abstract technical visualization called the "Hinge Meter" for a decision-support tool named CARDO GUARD.

**Overall aesthetic:**
The image must feel like a clean, sophisticated vector diagram from a high-quality engineering manual or technical decision document. Think precise physics force diagrams, analog instrumentation graphics, or Edward Tufte-style clarity. It should look serious, high-signal, and professional — not illustrative, not corporate-glossy, not playful, and not like a modern startup dashboard.

**Core structure (force diagram style):**
- A strong, thin central vertical line represents the immovable "hinge" (the decision pivot point).
- On the left side of the hinge: 3–4 fixed-width vertical bars of varying heights representing risks/costs (pulling the decision toward "DO NOT ACT").
- On the right side of the hinge: 3–4 fixed-width vertical bars of varying heights representing benefits/upside (pulling the decision toward "ACT").
- A thin, precise horizontal needle originates from the central hinge and points toward the heavier side.
- Directly beneath the needle’s travel path, render a clear horizontal gradient/shaded uncertainty zone centered on the hinge. This zone must visually communicate that the true decision boundary (hinge position) could land anywhere within that shaded range.

**Critical design rules (do not violate):**
- All bars must be strictly **fixed width** and only vary in **height**. Do not make them look like they are scaling in mass or width.
- Use a very subtle gradient fill on the bars that transitions from a neutral dark gray at the base to the side color at the top.
- Add extremely faint, low-opacity ghosted or dashed outlines behind each bar to indicate the uncertainty range of that individual weight (keep this very subtle).
- The uncertainty zone on the axis should be the **primary and most visible** way uncertainty is communicated. It should feel like a shaded "possible hinge landing area."
- The visualization must feel like a well-engineered mechanism or precision instrument — clean lines, high precision, restrained use of color, excellent visual hierarchy.

**Color direction:**
- Use a serious, technical palette.
- GO / Benefit side: Deep, muted green.
- NO-GO / Risk side: Deep, muted red (not bright or alarming).
- Hinge, needle, and structural elements: Dark charcoal or cool gray.
- Uncertainty zone: Subtle gradient that fades from the side colors toward neutral at the center.
- Overall background: Clean off-white or very dark neutral with high contrast.

**Style references (important):**
- Clean vector force diagrams from engineering or physics textbooks.
- High-end analog gauge or instrumentation graphics.
- Edward Tufte / serious information design aesthetic.
- Avoid rounded friendly shapes, heavy drop shadows, gradients that look digital/corporate, or any decorative elements.

**What to avoid:**
- Cartoonish or literal physical weights, chains, or gravity effects.
- Playful or overly illustrative styling.
- Startup dashboard aesthetics (thick lines, heavy rounding, bright colors, excessive glow).
- Making the visualization look like a generic balance scale or pros/cons diagram.

Generate a high-resolution, clean, professional version. Show the current decision state clearly (e.g. "ACT" or "DO NOT ACT") with excellent typography. Include a small, precise margin indicator.

Create 3 variations:
1. Moderate margin (needle clearly away from the hinge, strong decision).
2. High tension / close call (needle very near the hinge with large uncertainty zone).
3. A slightly more minimal version with fewer bars and stronger emphasis on the uncertainty zone on the axis.

---

**Tips for using this prompt:**
- After generating, you can follow up with refinements such as:
  - "Make the uncertainty zone on the axis even more prominent and gradient-based."
  - "Increase the precision and reduce any remaining illustrative qualities."
  - "Make the weights feel more like clean engineering bars with fixed width."
  - "Show a version with even more restrained, Tufte-inspired styling."
